These virtual entertainers are here to stay!

What are VTubers?

VTubers are a new type of virtual entertainers that are prevalent on live stream platforms such as YouTube, Twitch, etc. with ten of thousands of vtubers on these platforms everyday. Combined veiwership is in the millions and some of these virtual entertainers are earning millions of dollars a month.

Ever since the rise of vtubers, new ways to consume entertainment and audience particpation as followed, while live streaming as always been popular due to streamers themselves interacting with the audience while showing themselves with a face cam, vtubers are different.

VTubers use a animated virtual avatar, it can be two dimensional or three dimensional, to live stream. With how easy it is to stream, while protecting your privacy due to the fact that the streamer embodies their virtual avatar without having to ever show their face and sometimes not even use their voice. With the amount of open source and free resources, this medium has exploded from a trend into a legitimate industry.

While new in the entertainment sector, these virtual avatars, that are controlled by people in real-time, are rapidly growing and attracting large brands that provide these vtubers with resources and sponsorships to attract large audiences. Their popularity in Japan has expanded to establishing companies and agencies to, usually, bring a group together for the sake of entertainment like you would see in the music industry.

In fact, the VTuber industry does take inspiration from the music industry, especially in Japan, since many of these individuals or groups form idol groups and do attend or even perform concerts that will attract tens of thousands of people.

The first vtuber, at least in terms of what the definition of what a vtuber is currently, is Kizuna Ai. She first appeared in Japan as a YouTuber in 2016, early 2017 and quickly rose to prominence during 2018/2019, she is currently part of Kizuna AI Inc., a subsidiary of the digital entertainment company Activ8.
