The Technology that allows VTubing to exist

What is behind the scenes?

The Models

The one thing that makes a vtuber work is the model, however, since the independent scene is quite large there are a larger variety of different types of VTubers. In fact, there is the term PNGTuber, which is basically a person interacting with the audience as a PNG image, some streamers have even taken the concept and added reactions with a different set of images. However, the first instance of VTubers were using advanced 3D models, such a Ami Yamato and Kizuna Ai. Commissioning a 3D model can be a large investment, however, because of the community, open source software has allowed any person to get a 3D model working and ready to go in a matter of hours. VRoid Studio is a great example of a easy way to get in VTubing for any person.

Here is an example:

After 3D models, 2D models with advanced rigging came into the picture, at first, they were crude but quickly became the most popular form of VTuber model. This is due to the fact that, while it's even more expensive to commission a 2D model, it vastly saves on much needed computer resources. Expensive and high-end PCs aren't something that most people have, such a PC would allow them to interact with their trackers, sensors, render their 3d model in real-time while also live streaming at the same time, this would be very expensive on the CPU and GPU. 2D models have the advantage of using very little GPU performance, so it would allow them to stream at higher quality and play more graphically intensive games. Other resources like Unity and Blender help create and rig 3D models.

Tracking

Another important aspect of VTubing is the tracking, depending on the model, the tracking can either be complex and uses a motion tracker suit or even just using an iPhone camera to track. Some are so advanced that it can do whole body tracking with the model doing a one-to-one actions in real-time, Zentreya and Projekt Melody from VShojo comes to mind. Audience Particpation

What makes VTubing an interactive experience is the fact that you can directly interact with the virtual avatar, whether through new software just introduced to VTube Studio or getting on VRChat and interacting in virtual reality with the VTuber. Audience particpation plays a large part in this medium, without it, it more than likely would not have taken off in the West. There are many instances of the live stream chat collectively interacting with the VTuber through the use of the tools provided to them by the VTuber themselves. Froot from VShojo has a sound board that the chat can play if they donate enough money to play it, the chat in this case, annoys Froot with the soundboard until she dies in Crab Game (a Squid Game inspired game).

Virtual Reality

A large role that gave raise to the medium is VR. Virtual Reality allows VTubers to interact with people digitally and can create events that allow people to attend in real-time. Kizuna Ai, so far, always attempts to push the boundaries of virtual reality mixed with physical reality. Her recent concerts has her and other guests, either virtual or physcial on stage to sing and dance with her. For others, VR is used to hang out with their audience or friends, either to play games with either or talk to each other. Here is an example of VTubers Nyanners and Ironmouse of VShojo interacting in VR with each other and showing off to their audience.

The resources used by these VTubers has allowed the medium to grow at a rapid pace due to the audience particpation, this is followed by the expansion of the VTuber industry into music industry. Through the use of 3D Models, game engines like Unreal, and advanced full-body tracking has made possible large concerts and smaller events. The technology has matured so fast, that anybody can use the free resources available for use in order to express themselves and embody what they may preceive to be their true selves since they are free to design or commission any model they may free embodies thier personality.
